<p><p>The photoconductive cell market primarily includes two types: Cadmium Sulphide (CdS) and Cadmium Selenide (CdSe). CdS cells are widely used in light sensing applications due to their sensitivity to visible light, making them ideal for devices like light meters and automatic streetlights. CdSe cells, on the other hand, are known for their sensitivity in the infrared spectrum, making them suitable for applications in thermal imaging and telecommunications. Both materials play critical roles in various electronic and optoelectronic devices.</p></p>
