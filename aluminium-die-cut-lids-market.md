Soft aluminium alloys are known for their malleability and excellent formability, making them ideal for intricate designs and applications requiring easy manipulation. In contrast, half-hard aluminium alloys offer a balance between flexibility and strength, providing enhanced durability while maintaining moderate pliability. Both types are extensively used in packaging across various industries, including food and pharmaceuticals, due to their lightweight and corrosion-resistant properties.</p></p>
