<p><p>Gradebook software can be categorized into two primary market types: cloud-based and on-premises solutions. Cloud-based gradebook software operates on remote servers, allowing users to access their data from anywhere with an internet connection, which promotes collaboration and real-time updates. On-premises gradebook software, on the other hand, is installed locally on individual computers or servers, providing higher control over data security and customization but may require more maintenance and upfront costs. Each type has distinct advantages tailored to different institutional needs.</p></p>
